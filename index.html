Core Expertise

How I work

Before I write a spec or plan a sprint, I ask: are we solving the right problem? These are the things I try to get right on every project.

🔍
Customer Insight

I talk to operators and riders regularly — not just at the start of a project, but throughout. The best calls I've made came from a conversation, not a dashboard.

⚡
Differentiation & Competitive Thinking

I keep an eye on what competitors are doing, but I'm more interested in what they're not doing. That gap is usually where the real opportunity sits.

🎯
End-to-End Ownership

I stay involved from the first conversation about a problem through to seeing whether it actually worked. Handing off once something ships doesn't feel like ownership to me.

📊
Data & Metrics Ownership

I try to agree on what success looks like before anything gets built. It saves arguments later and keeps everyone honest — including me. I'm comfortable writing SQL and building dashboards to get to the answer myself.

🤝
Aligning Stakeholders

Getting engineering, hardware, ops, and leadership on the same page is genuinely hard. I spend a lot of time writing things down clearly and making trade-offs explicit so the same decision doesn't get relitigated three times.

Things I've worked on

Smart Vehicle Platform

Making Vehicles Intelligent at Joyride

Joyride · Connected Mobility

Before this, operators had no visibility into what vehicles were doing until something went wrong. We connected GEM Cars and Yamaha vehicles to the cloud so the team could actually see what was happening — and act on it — in real time.

🚗 GEM Car
🛺 Yamaha Car
Platform Strategy Hardware Integration Fleet Intelligence Vendor Coordination
🎯 Operators could check vehicle status remotely, diagnose issues faster, and start making decisions based on actual usage data instead of guesswork.
Telematics Integration

Fleet Telematics Data Pipeline at Joyride

Joyride · Platform Engineering

Different vehicle partners sent data in completely different formats — some through REST APIs, some over raw TCP connections. That made it really hard to build anything reliable on top. We built one shared data layer so the rest of the platform didn't have to care about those differences.

🔗 REST API
📶 Direct TCP
📡 MQTT
Data Platform Strategy Protocol Abstraction Operator Intelligence Fleet Analytics
🎯 Adding a new vehicle type became much faster, and GPS tracking, diagnostics, and usage analytics became available across the whole fleet through one consistent interface.
Diagnostic Tooling

Gopal Desk — Vehicle Diagnostic Platform

Internal Tool · Fleet Operations

As the fleet grew, figuring out what was wrong with a vehicle was taking too long. The support and ops team were piecing things together from raw logs and instinct. Gopal Desk was built to give anyone on the team the tools to properly understand a vehicle — what it's doing, what it was doing, and why it failed.

🔧 Vehicle Debugging
📋 Full Diagnostics
Internal Platform Strategy Operational Leverage CAN Data Support Tooling
🎯 Diagnosis time went down, the team got better at catching problems before they became serious, and we stopped depending on one or two people who knew how to read the raw data.
Motor Controller

Pride Scooter Controller — Rental Mode

Joyride · Hardware & Firmware

The same scooter needed to behave differently depending on whether it was being used as a rental or as a personally owned vehicle. Managing two separate hardware setups was getting messy. We worked with the Astra IoT device to add mode-switching at the controller level so operators could change the behaviour remotely, over the air.

🛵 Pride Scooter
📟 Astra Device
Business Model Enablement Fleet Flexibility Firmware Strategy SKU Reduction
🎯 One hardware configuration now covers both rental and personal use — which simplified procurement and made fleet management noticeably less complicated.

About Me

A bit about me

I'm a product manager who works closely with engineers, hardware teams, and operators. I've found that in products where hardware and software are tightly coupled, it really helps to understand both sides — not to do the engineering work, but to ask the right questions and know when something is genuinely hard.

Most of my work at Joyride has been in connected vehicles — getting vehicle data flowing reliably, building tools for operators to manage their fleets, and figuring out how to make new hardware work within existing systems.

I like working on problems that are still being figured out, where the right answer isn't obvious and where the decisions you make early actually matter later.
