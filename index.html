How I work

Before I write a spec or plan a sprint, I ask: are we solving the actual problem? These are the things I do for making sure the product is successful.

I talk to operators and riders regularly to understand the market not just at the start but throughout the product lifecycle. The best decisions I've made are derived from a conversation backed by data.

Documentation & Guided Support

Joyride Neon IoT — Conversational Installation Guide

Joyride · Installer & Customer Experience

The Neon IoT install manual was accurate for our hardware, but it assumed people were already comfortable with wiring checks and basic tools. In practice, hobbyists and enthusiasts often had simple questions we did not want to answer only with “hire a technician,” which added cost and friction. We turned the installation flow into a conversational guide so people could ask what to do next, step by step, and we linked out to supporting articles — things like how to use a multimeter — when the Joyride doc was not the right place to teach fundamentals.

💬 Conversational manual
📡 Neon IoT install
📎 Linked how-to articles
Self-Serve Enablement Content & Support Strategy Guided Install UX LLM-Style Assistance
🎯 Installers could stay in one flow for Neon-specific steps while still getting honest help on basics, without every question becoming a paid truck roll. The live product is not something I can demo here — but the quick-answers chat on this site is built in a similar spirit so you can get a feel for conversational, step-oriented help.
